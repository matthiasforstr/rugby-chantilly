## Product Overview

**One-liner:** The historic FFR-certified rugby club of Chantilly, training kids from age 4 to 16 and welcoming adults for non-contact Rugby 5.

**What it does:** R.O.C.C. is a local rugby association based at Stade des Bourgognes (Chantilly, Oise). The club runs an FFR-certified Rugby School (Baby/U6 → U14), a U16 Cadets team, and a Rugby Touché (Rugby 5) program for adults of any level. Founded in 1995, the club serves the Chantilly area as the local home for rugby practice, competition, and community.

**Product category:** Local sports club / Youth sports academy / Community rugby club

**Product type:** Non-profit association (loi 1901), FFR-affiliated

**Business model:** Annual membership fees (cotisations) + sponsorships + (likely) municipal subsidies. Free awareness, paid participation.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem (parents):**
"In Chantilly there are a lot of options for kids' sports — football, judo, tennis, equestrian — but I don't know which one is actually serious about coaching, safe, and a good community. And rugby specifically scares me a little."

**Core problem (adults):**
"I want to play rugby again but I'm 38 and I don't want to get hurt. Touch rugby exists but I don't know any club nearby that runs it for adults."

**Core problem (sponsors):**
"I want to support local sport but I don't want to just write a check and never hear about it again. I want my brand to be visible and the relationship to feel real."

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Football clubs** are easier to find but oversubscribed and impersonal.
- **Other rugby clubs in Oise** (Senlis, Compiègne, Beauvais) are further away and often less specialized in youth.
- **Equestrian** (a Chantilly classic) is expensive and elitist for many families.
- **Doing nothing / screens** — kids end up sedentary, parents feel guilty.

**What it costs them:**
- Parents: years of indecision, kids on screens, missed friendships.
- Adults: lost fitness, no real outdoor weekend social.
- Sponsors: marketing budget that disappears into Facebook ads with no community return.

**Emotional tension:**
- Parents: guilt + safety anxiety + decision fatigue.
- Adults: nostalgia + body-betrayal + not wanting to be "that old guy on the field".
- Sponsors: cynicism about local marketing + genuine desire to do something good.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors (other rugby clubs in the region):**
- Rugby Club de Senlis (~15 km) — closest neighbouring rugby club; falls short on Chantilly-local convenience
- Other rugby clubs in Oise / Hauts-de-France (Compiègne, Beauvais, broader region) — too far for weekly youth practice

**Secondary competitors (THE REAL COMPETITION — other ball sports for the same kid):**
- Football clubs in Chantilly / Gouvieux — by far the most popular default; easier, oversubscribed, less character-shaping
- Basketball / handball / volleyball clubs in the area
- Judo / martial arts — structured, indoor, individualistic
- Equestrian (Chantilly is the French capital of horse racing) — local, prestigious, but expensive
- Tennis Club de Chantilly — clean and proper, but country-club coded

> **Key insight:** the real competition is NOT other rugby clubs (they're too far). It's the other ball sports parents pick by default — especially football. Marketing should fight for *ball-sport mindshare*, not *rugby-club mindshare*.

**Indirect competitors (the real enemy):**
- "Rien" / "Trop loin" / "On verra l'année prochaine" — procrastination, screens, parents' own fatigue.

**How they fall short:**
- Other rugby clubs: distance from Chantilly is the killer.
- Football: oversubscribed, impersonal, less character-shaping.
- Equestrian: cost barrier.
- Doing nothing: real cost in fitness, social skills, family weekend energy.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
1. **Only ball-sport club in Chantilly that takes kids from U6 (age 4)** — football and most others start later. This is a unique on-ramp for very young children.
2. **FFR-certified Rugby School** — official label, not just "we have a kids team".
3. **Located IN Chantilly** at the Stade des Bourgognes — beats every "20 minutes away" competitor for the local family.
4. **Full age ladder Baby → U16** — the family doesn't have to switch clubs as the kid grows.
5. **Rugby 5 (Touch) for adults** — solves the adult "I want to play but not get hurt" problem that almost no neighbouring club addresses.
6. **30 years of history** (founded 1995, formerly Rugby Olympique-Club Cantilien).
7. **Active third-half tradition** ("3ème mi-temps") — the post-match social is part of the offer, not an afterthought. Real community, not just a training slot.
8. **Volunteer-run ("bénévolat")** — engaged parents are integrated into the club, with programs for coach training and roles for parents who want to contribute beyond drop-off/pick-up.

**How we do it differently:**
A small, family-driven local club (real dragon logo, real characters, real TikTok), not a big federation factory. We don't just take your kid — we welcome the whole family. Coaches are often parents who trained up. The third half is as important as the match.

**Why that's better:** Parents trust their kid is known by name AND find a place to belong themselves. Adults find a real team and a real bar afterwards. Sponsors back a community that meets in person every weekend.

**Why members choose us:**
- "C'est le seul club de sport à Chantilly qui prend mon enfant à 4 ans."
- "It's right here in Chantilly."
- "The coaches are FFR-certified."
- "My kid actually wants to come back — and so do we, the parents."
- "On reste pour la 3ème mi-temps."
- "I can play touch and not get a concussion."

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Rugby is too dangerous for my child" | Baby/U8/U10 are play-and-motor focused with progressive contact, FFR-certified coaches, full safety protocols. Statistically not more dangerous than skiing or judo at this age. |
| "I'm too old / too out of shape" | Touch rugby (Rugby 5) is no-contact, mixed, all levels. The fittest people on the pitch are often the ones who started at 40. |
| "We're not from a rugby family — we won't fit in" | The club is mostly first-time families. The whole point of the École de Rugby is to teach the game from zero, including parents. |
| "Why not football?" | Football is everywhere and crowded. Rugby teaches a specific kind of teamwork and physical literacy. The kids who try rugby tend to stay. |
| "We don't have time" | One weekly training session + a match. Less than most other sports. The U6 sessions are 45 minutes. |
| "I don't speak French well enough" | The club site and communication is being made bilingual. Rugby is largely a non-verbal sport on the pitch. |

**Anti-persona:**
- Hyper-elite parents looking for a "rugby academy" that produces pros — wrong club, go to a federal training centre.
- Adults wanting full-contact senior tackle league — R.O.C.C. doesn't field a senior tackle team currently.
- Sponsors looking for stadium-level visibility / national audience — this is local, not regional.

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Familial, collégial, warm, hands-on. We talk like a teammate, not a brand. Direct, honest, slightly old-school, never corporate.

**Style:** Short sentences. Concrete nouns. French-first. We talk about *the club*, *the team*, *the third half* — not "the experience" or "the product".

**Personality (5 adjectives):**
- Familial — the family-first identity is core, not decorative
- Collégial — the club is run by volunteers, players and parents alike
- Heritage (founded 1995, club tradition)
- Honest (no marketing fluff, no hype)
- Slightly understated (Chantilly elegance, not American-sports hype)

**Voice rules:**
- We don't say "join our community" — we say "viens prendre une bière après le match".
- We don't say "elite training" — we say "des coachs qui connaissent ton gamin par son prénom".
- We mention the **3ème mi-temps** explicitly — it's a differentiator, not a cliché.
- We mention **bénévolat** as an offer, not just a need: there's a place for parents who want to coach, organize, communicate, drive a van.
